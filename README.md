# 📊 HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard

## 🎯 Objective

Analyze employee attrition patterns using the IBM HR Analytics dataset to identify workforce turnover drivers and support data-driven HR decision-making.

---

## 🛠 Tools Used

* SQL
* Power BI
* DAX
* Excel

---

## 📂 Dataset

IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset

Employees: 1,470

---

## 📊 Business Questions Addressed

* Which departments experience the highest attrition?
* Which age groups are most likely to leave?
* How does overtime affect attrition?
* What is the relationship between tenure and attrition?
* Which job roles experience the highest turnover?
* How does job satisfaction influence employee retention?

---

## 📈 Dashboard Features

* KPI Cards

  * Total Employees
  * Attrition Count
  * Attrition Rate
  * Average Salary
  * Average Age
  * Average Tenure

* Department-Level Analysis

  * Human Resources
  * Research & Development
  * Sales

* Attrition Analysis By

  * Age Group
  * Tenure
  * Education Field
  * Business Travel
  * Job Role
  * Gender
  * Overtime

* Interactive Department Filters

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

* Overall attrition rate stands at 16.1%.
* Employees working overtime show significantly higher attrition risk.
* Employees within their first 2 years demonstrate the highest turnover levels.
* Research & Development department records the highest attrition count.
* Sales department shows a high attrition proportion relative to workforce size.
* Employees aged 26–35 represent the largest attrition segment.
* Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives experience the highest employee turnover.
* Lower job satisfaction levels are associated with increased attrition.
